We invite you to participate in a research study on language production and comprehension. Your experimenter will ask you to do a linguistic task such as reading sentences or words, naming pictures or describing scenes, making up sentences of your own, or participating in a simple language game.

There are no risks or benefits of any kind involved in this study.

You will be paid for your participation at the posted rate.

If you have read this form and have decided to participate in this experiment, please understand your participation is voluntary and you have the right to withdraw your consent or discontinue participation at any time without penalty or loss of benefits to which you are otherwise entitled. Instructions

In this experiment, you will hear a talker say nonsense syllables, like ub or id or ack. Your job is to tell us what the last sound of each syllable is.

To do this, place your fingers on the S and K keys. If the sound you heard matches the letter on the left, press the S key. If the sound you heard matches the letter on the right, press the K key.

You will hear a lot of syllables that sound quite similar.

Do your best, and respond as quickly and accurately as you can, identifying the last sound of the syllable.

Please place your fingers on the S and K buttons, and follow the prompts to make your choices.

Once a selection is made, there will be a brief pause and then the slide will advance.
